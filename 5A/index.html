üëÅÔ∏è‚Äçüó®Ô∏è 1.8 Visual Anagrams

Visual anagrams are optical illusions created using diffusion models. These images change meaning when flipped upside down. The technique involves denoising an image with two different prompts simultaneously - one for the upright view and another for the flipped view. The resulting noise estimates are combined to create an image that represents both concepts.

‚ú® Analysis

Visual anagrams demonstrate the powerful ability of diffusion models to encode multiple concepts into a single image. By simultaneously denoising with two different prompts and orientations, we can create compelling optical illusions that reveal different interpretations when viewed normally versus upside down. This technique leverages the high-dimensional nature of the diffusion model's latent space to encode multiple semantic meanings in the same image region.

The quality of the illusions depends heavily on the relationship between the two prompts. Pairs with some visual similarities (like the Swan-Wolf example, both having similar shapes in the water/sky) tend to produce better results than completely unrelated concepts.

üß¨ 1.9 Hybrid Images

Hybrid images combine the low frequencies of one image with the high frequencies of another, creating a single image that appears as one concept when viewed up close and another when viewed from a distance. In this section, we use diffusion models to create hybrid images by combining noise estimates from two different text prompts - one contributing low frequencies and the other contributing high frequencies.

The algorithm works by:

  1. Generating noise estimates using two different text prompts via Classifier-Free Guidance
  2. Applying a low-pass filter to the first noise estimate to extract low frequencies
  3. Applying a high-pass filter to the second noise estimate to extract high frequencies
  4. Combining these filtered noise estimates to create the final hybrid image

Cat (low) + Forests (high)

Low frequencies prompt: "A big cartoon orange cat face at the center of image with white background"
High frequencies prompt: "Green forests"

Cat(low) + Forests(high)

Whale (low) + Mountains (high)

Low frequencies prompt: "A big cartoon whale at the center of image with white background"
High frequencies prompt: "Rock mountain peaks"

Whale(low) + Mountains(high)

‚ú® Analysis

Creating hybrid images with diffusion models demonstrates how semantic information can be separated and recombined in the latent noise space. By filtering noise estimates from different prompts, we can create images that contain semantic elements from both prompts.

The success of hybrid images depends on the compatibility of the two prompts. Prompts that have some conceptual overlap or complementary spatial structures tend to produce better results. The filtering process (using Gaussian blur with kernel size 33 and sigma 3) is crucial for separating frequency components effectively.

Unlike traditional image-based hybrid techniques, this approach works in the semantic latent space of the diffusion model, allowing for more creative and abstract combinations that wouldn't be possible with pixel-based methods alone.
